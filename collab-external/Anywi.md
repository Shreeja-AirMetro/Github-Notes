How difficult it is to get EU horizon grant ? 
- Highly competitive
- 15.9% success rate  - roughly 7 out of 10 application gets rejected despite meeting quality standards
- rigorous evaluation focused on scientific excellence, societal impact, and implementation feasibility
- Strict alignment - proposals must directly match the call's destinations (impact goals) and topic specific outcomes 
- Budget realism - projects risk rejection if costs are inadequately justified or exceed program limits
- 1. **Eligibility Check (Part A)**: Administrative verification by the European Commission[4](https://eufunds.me/how-are-horizon-europe-proposals-evaluated/).
- 2. **Technical Evaluation (Part B)**: External experts score proposals on three criteria[4](https://eufunds.me/how-are-horizon-europe-proposals-evaluated/)[8](https://ec.europa.eu/info/funding-tenders/opportunities/docs/2021-2027/experts/standard-briefing-slides-for-experts_he_en.pdf):
    - **Excellence** (50% weight): Scientific ambition, innovation, and methodological rigor.
    - **Impact** (30% weight): Societal, economic, or policy relevance, including scalability and sustainability.
    - **Quality & Efficiency** (20% weight): Realistic work plans, risk management, and consortium expertise.

Each criterion receives a score of 0–5, with thresholds often requiring ≥4 in Excellence and ≥3 in other sections[4](https://eufunds.me/how-are-horizon-europe-proposals-evaluated/). Evaluators prioritize proposals that align with EU policy goals (e.g., green transition, digital transformation)
## Strategies for Writing a Winning Proposal
Additional reference: 
- https://www.pnoconsultants.com/news/succes-rates-horizon-europe-rise-sharply/
- https://research-and-innovation.ec.europa.eu/funding/funding-opportunities/funding-programmes-and-open-calls/horizon-europe/eu-missions-horizon-europe/assessment-criteria_en

**1. Deeply Analyze the Work Programme**
- Identify the call’s expected outcomes and evaluation criteria[1](https://rea.ec.europa.eu/news/common-mistakes-avoid-when-applying-horizon-europe-funding-2023-02-09_en)[5](https://unite4horizon.eu/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/Write-a-winning-proposal.pdf).
- Review funded projects on CORDIS to understand successful approaches[5](https://unite4horizon.eu/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/Write-a-winning-proposal.pdf).


**2. Structure for Excellence**
- Define a **novel methodology** with measurable objectives[7](https://www.neto-innovation.com/post/expert-guide-to-writing-the-excellence-part-of-a-horizon-europe-proposal).
- Highlight consortium expertise through publications, patents, or prior projects[7](https://www.neto-innovation.com/post/expert-guide-to-writing-the-excellence-part-of-a-horizon-europe-proposal).
- Integrate open science, gender dimensions, and ethical AI practices[7](https://www.neto-innovation.com/post/expert-guide-to-writing-the-excellence-part-of-a-horizon-europe-proposal).


**3. Maximize Impact**
- Quantify outcomes (e.g., "Reduce CO₂ emissions by 20% by 2030")[6](https://kaila.eu/blog/horizon-europe-project/).
- Outline exploitation plans for post-project sustainability[6](https://kaila.eu/blog/horizon-europe-project/).
- Link results to EU policies like the European Green Deal[5](https://unite4horizon.eu/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/Write-a-winning-proposal.pdf)[8](https://ec.europa.eu/info/funding-tenders/opportunities/docs/2021-2027/experts/standard-briefing-slides-for-experts_he_en.pdf).

**4. Ensure Implementation Feasibility**
- Design a detailed work plan with clear milestones and risk-mitigation strategies[4](https://eufunds.me/how-are-horizon-europe-proposals-evaluated/)[8](https://ec.europa.eu/info/funding-tenders/opportunities/docs/2021-2027/experts/standard-briefing-slides-for-experts_he_en.pdf).
- Justify budgets using hourly rates and task-specific resource allocations[1](https://rea.ec.europa.eu/news/common-mistakes-avoid-when-applying-horizon-europe-funding-2023-02-09_en)[6](https://kaila.eu/blog/horizon-europe-project/).

**5. Refine Presentation**
- Use concise, jargon-free language and visual aids (e.g., Gantt charts)[6](https://kaila.eu/blog/horizon-europe-project/).
- Avoid formatting errors; adhere strictly to page limits and font guidelines[1](https://rea.ec.europa.eu/news/common-mistakes-avoid-when-applying-horizon-europe-funding-2023-02-09_en)[6](https://kaila.eu/blog/horizon-europe-project/).

Proposals that balance innovation with practical implementation, while directly addressing EU strategic priorities, have the highest success rates. Continuous iteration based on past evaluations and expert feedback is critical to refining competitive applications

## Strategies to Make Your Proposal Stand Out

**1. Demonstrate True Excellence and Innovation**
- Clearly articulate what is novel about your project and how it advances the state-of-the-art. Show that your work is ambitious, original, and scientifically or technologically significant[1](https://www.neto-innovation.com/post/expert-guide-to-writing-the-excellence-part-of-a-horizon-europe-proposal)[4](https://www.meta-group.com/news/10-tips-to-write-a-successful-eu-proposal/).
- Provide a detailed, robust methodology and work plan, highlighting how your approach is superior to existing solutions and how it will deliver the promised results[1](https://www.neto-innovation.com/post/expert-guide-to-writing-the-excellence-part-of-a-horizon-europe-proposal).

**2. Align Precisely with the Call’s Objectives**
- Thoroughly analyze the specific Horizon Europe topic and expected outcomes. Explicitly demonstrate how your project will deliver on these expectations and contribute to EU-level strategies and policies[1](https://www.neto-innovation.com/post/expert-guide-to-writing-the-excellence-part-of-a-horizon-europe-proposal)[2](https://unite4horizon.eu/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/Write-a-winning-proposal.pdf)[6](https://europamediatrainings.com/webinars/349/what-are-the-evaluators-looking-for-in-your-horizon-europe-proposal).
- Use the language and keywords from the call text to show evaluators your proposal is “within scope”[6](https://europamediatrainings.com/webinars/349/what-are-the-evaluators-looking-for-in-your-horizon-europe-proposal).


**3. Quantify and Emphasize Impact**
- Go beyond the expected impacts by identifying additional, measurable benefits your project will deliver—social, economic, environmental, or policy-related[7](https://www.fasttrackimpact.com/post/2017/10/31/how-to-write-a-horizon-2020-proposal-that-impresses-on-impact).
- Set clear, SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) to track progress and impact[1](https://www.neto-innovation.com/post/expert-guide-to-writing-the-excellence-part-of-a-horizon-europe-proposal)[7](https://www.fasttrackimpact.com/post/2017/10/31/how-to-write-a-horizon-2020-proposal-that-impresses-on-impact).


**4. Build a High-Quality, Diverse Consortium**
- Assemble a consortium with top experts and all necessary disciplines, including SMEs and partners from different countries. Diversity in expertise, geography, and gender can be decisive if proposals are tied in score[5](https://noldus.com/blog/tips-better-horizon-proposals)[6](https://europamediatrainings.com/webinars/349/what-are-the-evaluators-looking-for-in-your-horizon-europe-proposal).
- Clearly describe each partner’s unique contribution and how the consortium’s combined expertise is essential to the project’s success[5](https://noldus.com/blog/tips-better-horizon-proposals).


**5. Address Cross-Cutting and Horizontal Issues**
- Integrate open science, gender dimension, ethics, and social sciences/humanities where relevant[1](https://www.neto-innovation.com/post/expert-guide-to-writing-the-excellence-part-of-a-horizon-europe-proposal)[6](https://europamediatrainings.com/webinars/349/what-are-the-evaluators-looking-for-in-your-horizon-europe-proposal).
- Explicitly address “Do No Significant Harm” (DNSH) principles and, if using AI, discuss robustness and ethics[1](https://www.neto-innovation.com/post/expert-guide-to-writing-the-excellence-part-of-a-horizon-europe-proposal).


**6. Craft a Convincing, Cohesive Narrative**
- Make your proposal clear, concise, and logically structured. Each section should reinforce the others, building a consistent story of excellence, impact, and feasibility[2](https://unite4horizon.eu/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/Write-a-winning-proposal.pdf)[4](https://www.meta-group.com/news/10-tips-to-write-a-successful-eu-proposal/).
- Use visual aids (Gantt charts, diagrams) and avoid jargon or unnecessary complexity[4](https://www.meta-group.com/news/10-tips-to-write-a-successful-eu-proposal/).

**7. Plan for Stakeholder Engagement and Exploitation**
- Identify and involve key stakeholders from the start. Present a credible plan for how results will be exploited, disseminated, and sustained after the project ends[7](https://www.fasttrackimpact.com/post/2017/10/31/how-to-write-a-horizon-2020-proposal-that-impresses-on-impact).
- Show how your project will create value for society, industry, and policymakers[7](https://www.fasttrackimpact.com/post/2017/10/31/how-to-write-a-horizon-2020-proposal-that-impresses-on-impact).
**8. Highlight Your Track Record**
- Showcase relevant publications, patents, and successful past projects to demonstrate your team’s capability to deliver results[1](https://www.neto-innovation.com/post/expert-guide-to-writing-the-excellence-part-of-a-horizon-europe-proposal).

## Key Questions to Answer in Your Proposal
- Why is this project needed now?
- What is unique about your approach?
- Why is your consortium the best to deliver this?
- How will you ensure lasting impact beyond the project’s lifetime?

By following these strategies—demonstrating innovation, aligning tightly with the call, quantifying impact, building a strong consortium, and crafting a compelling narrative—you will significantly increase your chances of standing out and securing Horizon Europe funding[1](https://www.neto-innovation.com/post/expert-guide-to-writing-the-excellence-part-of-a-horizon-europe-proposal)[4](https://www.meta-group.com/news/10-tips-to-write-a-successful-eu-proposal/)[6](https://europamediatrainings.com/webinars/349/what-are-the-evaluators-looking-for-in-your-horizon-europe-proposal)[7](https://www.fasttrackimpact.com/post/2017/10/31/how-to-write-a-horizon-2020-proposal-that-impresses-on-impact).

Horizon project types and funding plan 

## **RIA — Research and Innovation Action**

- **Goal:**  
    Early-stage research, technology exploration, fundamental studies, prototypes, testing feasibility.
- **Funding rate:**  
    **100%** of eligible costs (for all types of participants — universities, SMEs, companies).
    
- **Typical activities:**
    - New knowledge generation
    - Proof-of-concept
    - Small-scale testing
    - Technology Readiness Level (TRL) usually from **3 to 5**
- **Example:**
    
    - Develop and test a new communication protocol for UAVs that could later be standardized.
    - Research into drone swarm control using AI.
        

## 2. **IA — Innovation Action**

- **Goal:**  
    Advance already known technologies closer to the market — larger-scale pilots, demonstrations, validation.
    
- **Funding rate:**
    
    - **70%** of eligible costs for companies (profit-oriented organizations)
    - **100%** for universities, non-profits, research centers.
        
- **Typical activities:**
    
    - Large pilots and real-world trials
    - Validation of new solutions in operational environments
    - Pre-commercial prototypes
    - TRL from **5 to 7 or 8**

- **Example:**
    
    - Large-scale deployment of UAV-based communication relays across several European cities for urban monitoring.
    - Integrating UAV communication modules with 5G networks in live tests.
        



## 3. **CSA — Coordination and Support Action**

- **Goal:**  
    Support actions, not direct research or technology development.
- **Funding rate:**  
    **100%** of eligible costs (everyone).
- **Typical activities:**
    
    - Networking
    - Policy support
    - Standards definition
    - Awareness-raising
    - Road-mapping
    - Stakeholder consultations
- **Example:**
    
    - Create a European platform to align standards for UAV-to-ground communication.
    - Organize workshops with authorities, airspace regulators, and drone operators across Europe.
        



 Quick Way to Remember:

|Type|Focus|Funding|
|---|---|---|
|**RIA**|Research, exploration, early-stage innovation|100%|
|**IA**|Market-ready pilots, demonstrations, late-stage development|70% (companies) / 100% (non-profits)|
|**CSA**|Coordination, policy support, standardization, no tech dev|100%|

## How to Make the Idea Strong

To maximize your proposal’s competitiveness:

- **Demonstrate Societal Value:** Clearly articulate the benefits for citizens, such as faster emergency medical deliveries, reduced congestion, and improved access in peri-urban areas[2](https://errin.eu/system/files/2024-04/POLICY%20PAPER%20ON%20DRONES.pdf).
    
- **Involve Stakeholders:** Engage city authorities, healthcare providers, logistics companies, and citizens early in the project design. Multi-level governance and stakeholder buy-in are essential[1](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11).
    
- **Robust Safety and Security Measures:** Detail your approach for safe BVLOS operations, including risk mitigation, traffic management, and integration with manned aviation[3](https://www.icas.org/icas_archive/icas2024/data/papers/icas2024_0347_paper.pdf).
    
- **Infrastructure Roadmap:** Prepare a detailed roadmap for the necessary infrastructure (vertiports, charging stations, digital corridors), and collaborate with city planners to integrate these into urban mobility plans[1](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11).
    
- **Demonstration Activities:** Plan ambitious, real-world demonstration flights with comprehensive data collection and monitoring to validate safety, efficiency, and societal acceptance[1](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11)[3](https://www.icas.org/icas_archive/icas2024/data/papers/icas2024_0347_paper.pdf).
    
- **Noise and Environmental Monitoring:** Include plans for noise monitoring and compliance, as well as assessments of environmental impact[1](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11).
    
- **Business Model and Replicability:** Develop viable business models and provide recommendations for replication in other cities, including training and competence-building for stakeholders[1](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11).
    
- **Societal Readiness and Communication:** Address societal readiness by including social sciences and humanities (SSH) expertise to facilitate public engagement, awareness campaigns, and address societal concerns

# EU Drone Logistics Projects: Comprehensive Overview

## Executive Summary

The European Union has established an extensive **research and innovation ecosystem** for drone logistics, with **24 major projects** identified across multiple funding programmes. These initiatives represent over **€235 million** in identified EU funding, spanning cargo delivery, medical logistics, urban air mobility, agricultural applications, and infrastructure maintenance. The projects are classified under both **Research and Innovation Actions (RIA)** and **Innovation Actions (IA)**, with additional support from the European Defence Industrial Development Programme (EDIDP) and European Investment Bank (EIB).

## Project Classification by Type

## Research and Innovation Actions (RIA) - 13 Projects

RIA projects focus on advancing technology readiness levels (TRL 2-6) through fundamental research and prototyping:

- **LABYRINTH**: Urban drone traffic management and logistics integration[1](https://www.dlr.de/en/fl/research-transfer/projects/labyrinth)[2](https://www.uasvision.com/2020/07/13/europe-creates-a-e5m-labyrinth-to-manage-low-level-drone-operations/)
    
- **CITYAM**: Urban air mobility planning and healthcare logistics[3](https://smartriga.lv/projects_cases/cityam-preparing-cities-for-sustainable-urban-air-mobility/)[4](https://interreg-baltic.eu/project/cityam/)
    
- **ICAERUS**: Multi-purpose agricultural, forestry, and rural logistics applications[5](https://business-school.open.ac.uk/research/research-activity/rural-economy-cluster/blogs/icaerus-supporting-sectoral-and-societal)[6](https://cordis.europa.eu/project/id/101060643)
    
- **SAFIR-Med**: Medical logistics and U-space integration[7](https://www.sesarju.eu/news/safir-med-completes-first-series-medical-delivery-drone-trials)
    
- **AIRCARRUS**: Autonomous drone delivery infrastructure system[8](https://cordis.europa.eu/article/id/415490-building-the-world-s-drone-infrastructure)[9](https://cordis.europa.eu/project/id/867479/reporting)

## Key Funding Programmes

## Horizon Europe and Horizon 2020

The **largest funding source** with 11 projects representing the EU's commitment to civilian drone logistics research. Projects span from agricultural applications (ICAERUS, SPADE) to medical logistics (SAFIR-Ready) and urban air mobility (CITYAM)[35](https://publications.jrc.ec.europa.eu/repository/bitstream/JRC137574/JRC137574_01.pdf).

## SESAR Joint Undertaking

Focuses on **U-space development** and air traffic management integration, funding critical projects like U-ELCOME and SAFIR-Med that demonstrate real-world logistics applications[23](https://www.sesarju.eu/news/u-elcome)[7](https://www.sesarju.eu/news/safir-med-completes-first-series-medical-delivery-drone-trials).

## European Defence Fund and EDIDP

Supports **dual-use technologies** and military applications with over €100 million allocated to projects like Eurodrone, addressing both defence needs and potential civilian logistics applications[36](https://www.defensenews.com/global/europe/2025/05/01/european-defence-fund-funnels-money-to-drones-hypersonic-defense-ai/)[26](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/RegData/etudes/BRIE/2025/772885/EPRS_BRI\(2025\)772885_EN.pdf).

## European Investment Bank

Provides **commercial-scale funding** exemplified by the €40 million investment in Wingcopter, bridging the gap between research and market deployment[33](https://wingcopter.com/eib).

## Geographic Distribution and Scope

Projects span across **all EU member states** with significant concentrations in:

- **Germany**: Leading in offshore logistics, medical delivery, and manufacturing
    
- **France, Spain, Italy**: Major participants in multi-country consortiums
    
- **Nordic countries**: Pioneering urban air mobility and emergency services
    
- **Benelux**: Centers for U-space development and testing[4](https://interreg-baltic.eu/project/cityam/)[23](https://www.sesarju.eu/news/u-elcome)

## Current Status and Timeline

**17 projects are currently ongoing**, demonstrating the active nature of EU drone logistics development. **7 projects have been completed**, providing valuable lessons and technological foundations for current initiatives. The concentration of ongoing projects indicates that **2024-2027** represents a critical period for European drone logistics maturation.

## Strategic Impact and Innovation

The EU's approach emphasizes **interoperability, safety, and societal acceptance** through:

- **U-space integration**: Ensuring safe coexistence with manned aviation
    
- **Cross-border harmonization**: Enabling seamless European logistics networks
    
- **Dual-use technology development**: Leveraging defence investments for civilian applications
    
- **SME participation**: Supporting innovation ecosystem development with 27% of EDF funding going to small and medium enterprises[36](https://www.defensenews.com/global/europe/2025/05/01/european-defence-fund-funnels-money-to-drones-hypersonic-defense-ai/)
    

## Future Outlook

These projects collectively position Europe as a **global leader in drone logistics regulation and technology**. The comprehensive funding approach, spanning from fundamental research to commercial deployment, creates a robust pipeline for transforming European logistics infrastructure. The emphasis on medical applications, agricultural sustainability, and urban mobility aligns with broader EU policy objectives including the **Green Deal** and **Digital Transformation** strategies[40](https://transport.ec.europa.eu/transport-modes/air/aviation-safety/unmanned-aircraft-drones_en)[41](https://transport.ec.europa.eu/news-events/news/drone-strategy-creating-large-scale-european-drone-market-2022-11-29_en).

The identified projects represent a **coordinated European effort** to establish technological sovereignty in drone logistics while addressing critical societal needs from healthcare delivery to sustainable agriculture and emergency response capabilities.

# EU Projects That Achieved Last-Mile Delivery Concepts

Several groundbreaking projects have successfully implemented last-mile delivery concepts using drones across the EU, with different companies and initiatives reaching operational status at varying scales.

## Manna Air Delivery: The Dublin Success Story

**Manna** has achieved the most comprehensive last-mile delivery implementation in the EU through its Dublin operations[1](https://www.irishexaminer.com/business/arid-30984127.html)[2](https://retail-systems.com/rs/Just_eat_rolls_out_drone_operated_deliveries_in_dublin.php)[3](https://www.thegrocer.co.uk/news/just-eat-launches-drone-deliveries-in-dublin/701835.article). Operating from bases in **Blanchardstown** and **Dublin 15**, Manna has completed over **200,000 deliveries**[4](https://dronexl.co/2025/04/12/manna-aero-drone-delivery-revolution/) and reached more than **20% of Dublin 15's population**[5](https://www.rte.ie/news/regional/2025/0209/1495588-drone-delivery-dublin/). The service achieves delivery times of **just 3 minutes** from ordering to doorstep[1](https://www.irishexaminer.com/business/arid-30984127.html)[2](https://retail-systems.com/rs/Just_eat_rolls_out_drone_operated_deliveries_in_dublin.php), with drones flying at speeds up to **80 km/h**[6](https://www.nova.ie/deliveroo-launches-world-first-drone-delivery-service-in-dublin-267153/)[7](https://www.retailgazette.co.uk/blog/2025/06/deliveroo-drone-deliveries/).

Manna's success has enabled partnerships with major food delivery platforms including **Just Eat** (launched March 2025)[2](https://retail-systems.com/rs/Just_eat_rolls_out_drone_operated_deliveries_in_dublin.php)[3](https://www.thegrocer.co.uk/news/just-eat-launches-drone-deliveries-in-dublin/701835.article) and **Deliveroo** (launched June 2025)[6](https://www.nova.ie/deliveroo-launches-world-first-drone-delivery-service-in-dublin-267153/)[7](https://www.retailgazette.co.uk/blog/2025/06/deliveroo-drone-deliveries/). The company operates **five large white drones** making up to **300 deliveries daily** within a **3km radius**[5](https://www.rte.ie/news/regional/2025/0209/1495588-drone-delivery-dublin/), covering approximately **150,000 people** in the Blanchardstown area[4](https://dronexl.co/2025/04/12/manna-aero-drone-delivery-revolution/).

The company's achievement extends beyond food delivery, handling **medicine, books, and consumer goods**[8](https://retail-systems.com/rs/Deliveroo_launches_drone_food_deliveries_in_dublin.php)[9](https://nationaltechnology.co.uk/Just_eat_rolls_out_drone_operated_deliveries_in_dublin.php). Manna operates under **EASA (European Union Aviation Safety Agency)** regulations[2](https://retail-systems.com/rs/Just_eat_rolls_out_drone_operated_deliveries_in_dublin.php)[9](https://nationaltechnology.co.uk/Just_eat_rolls_out_drone_operated_deliveries_in_dublin.php), providing a regulatory framework for expansion across other EU countries[9](https://nationaltechnology.co.uk/Just_eat_rolls_out_drone_operated_deliveries_in_dublin.php)[10](https://retailtechinnovationhub.com/home/2025/3/6/just-eat-takeawaycom-drone-delivery-partnership-with-manna-drone-delivery-kicks-off-in-dublin-ireland).

## Amazon Prime Air: European Pioneer Testing

**Amazon** conducted the **first successful European drone delivery test** on December 4, 2024, in **San Salvo, Italy**[11](https://dig.watch/updates/italy-hosts-amazons-first-european-drone-trial)[12](https://tech.co/news/amazon-drone-delivery-european-test)[13](https://www.aerospacetestinginternational.com/news/amazon-poised-to-launch-european-drone-delivery-service-after-italy-flight-tests.html). Using the advanced **MK-30 drone**, Amazon demonstrated autonomous delivery capabilities with **computer vision obstacle avoidance** and **24-kilometer range**[11](https://dig.watch/updates/italy-hosts-amazons-first-european-drone-trial)[12](https://tech.co/news/amazon-drone-delivery-european-test). The MK-30 can deliver packages **up to 2.5 kilograms**, covering approximately **90% of Amazon's product catalog**[13](https://www.aerospacetestinginternational.com/news/amazon-poised-to-launch-european-drone-delivery-service-after-italy-flight-tests.html)[14](https://www.customerservicemanager.com/its-a-bird-its-a-plane-its-my-drone-delivery/).

Amazon plans to launch commercial operations in **Italy and the UK by late 2024**[11](https://dig.watch/updates/italy-hosts-amazons-first-european-drone-trial)[13](https://www.aerospacetestinginternational.com/news/amazon-poised-to-launch-european-drone-delivery-service-after-italy-flight-tests.html), with the service already operational in **Texas and Arizona** since 2022. The European expansion represents Amazon's **first international deployment** of its Prime Air technology[15](https://dronexl.co/2024/12/07/amazon-drone-delivery-europe-italian-test-flight/)[16](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/dronexl_amazon-takes-first-steps-toward-drone-delivery-activity-7271183086405533696-hcZf).

## Wingcopter: Rural Last-Mile Innovation

The system combines **drone delivery to landing points** outside villages with **electric cargo bike** final delivery to homes[19](https://wingcopter.com/odenwald_liefermichel)[20](https://www.autoevolution.com/news/pilot-delivery-project-in-germany-brings-together-drones-and-e-cargo-bikes-222431.html). - Develop **proactive community engagement frameworks** that address privacy concerns through transparent data governance
    
- Create **participatory design processes** where communities co-create drone service parameters
    
- Implement **dynamic consent mechanisms** allowing residents to opt-in/out of specific drone operations over their areas

## **Phase 1: Community Co-Design (6 months)**

- Establish **participatory design workshops** in target communities
    
- Develop **community-specific use cases** based on local needs
    
- Create **transparent governance structures** for ongoing community involvement
    

## **Phase 2: Technology Integration (12 months)**

- Build **modular drone platforms** adaptable to multiple specialized applications
    
- Develop **blockchain integration** for supply chain transparency
    
- Implement **AI-powered predictive capabilities**
    

## **Phase 3: Regulatory Innovation (18 months)**

- Launch **regulatory sandbox partnerships** with multiple national authorities
    
- Test **cross-border automated compliance systems**
    
- Establish **precedent-setting legal frameworks** for community-owned drone infrastructure
    

## **Phase 4: Market Differentiation (24 months)**

- Deploy **first-of-kind specialized services** (cultural heritage, circular economy)
    
- Demonstrate **measurable community benefits** and acceptance
    
- Scale **carbon-negative delivery networks** across multiple EU regions

Your proposal's uniqueness will be sustainable because it addresses **systemic gaps** rather than technological features:

1. **Social acceptance challenges** that all drone logistics companies face
    
2. **Regulatory complexity** that creates barriers for all operators
    
3. **Specialized market needs** that remain unmet by current general-purpose solutions
    
4. **Sustainability requirements** that will only become more stringent
    

By focusing on these structural challenges rather than just technological innovation, your proposal creates **defensible competitive advantages** that cannot be easily replicated by existing players.

The key to success lies in **combining multiple unique elements** rather than relying on a single differentiator, creating a **holistic value proposition** that transforms how European communities engage with drone logistics technology while delivering measurable social, environmental, and economic benefits.

# Innovative Strategies to Address EU Defense and Delivery Drone Challenges

## 1. Harmonised Dual-Use Regulatory Framework

**Objective**: Bridge the civilian–military regulatory divide to accelerate deployment of both defense and delivery drones.

- Establish a single “Drone Operations Platform” that integrates EASA’s U-space rules with the EDF’s counter-drone protocols, enabling rapid mutual recognition of safety, airworthiness, and cybersecurity certifications across civil and military domains[1](https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/api/files/document/print/en/ip_22_7076/IP_22_7076_EN.pdf)[2](https://epthinktank.eu/2025/06/04/military-drone-systems-in-the-eu-and-global-context-types-capabilities-and-regulatory-frameworks/).
    
- Deploy an EU-wide “Reg-as-a-Service” API for pre-flight checks and cross-border authorisations, reducing approval times from weeks to under 72 hours through regulatory sandbox agreements with at least two national aviation authorities[3](https://institutdelors.eu/en/publications/a-rampart-of-drones-to-defend-europe/).
    

## 2. Modular Open-Architecture & Interoperability Standards

**Objective**: Ensure seamless integration, resilience, and rapid scalability of heterogeneous drone fleets.

- Adopt the EDA’s INTERACT interoperability standard as the basis for a “Common Drone Bus,” providing plug-and-play interfaces for payloads (sensors, counter-UAS modules) and control stations in both defense and logistic fleets[4](https://armyrecognition.com/archives/archives-land-defense/land-defense-2023/european-defence-agency-led-project-shapes-standards-for-unmanned-systems).
    
- Mandate open, MOSAIC-style gateways (as pioneered by Airbus–Quantum Systems) for AI-driven mission management, allowing frontline forces and commercial operators to share secure situational data and flight corridors while retaining sovereign control of sensitive functionalities[5](https://dronelife.com/2025/06/17/airbus-quantum-systems-defense-drone-partnership/).
    

## 3. AI-Enabled Dynamic Airspace & Counter-Drone Copilot

**Objective**: Enhance safety, mission assurance, and resilience against emerging threats.

- Develop an EU-funded “Drone Copilot” toolkit embedding AI for real-time tactical de-confliction in U-space, dynamic rerouting around extreme weather or contested zones, and automated engagement of counter-drone defences when non-cooperative UAVs are detected[6](https://joint-research-centre.ec.europa.eu/projects-and-activities/drones-counter-drones-and-autonomous-systems_en).
    
- Integrate predictive maintenance and demand-forecasting algorithms to optimise sortie rates, payload readiness, and lifecycle management, drawing on practices from delivery network demand forecasts and EU defence readiness studies[7](https://core.ac.uk/download/616563931.pdf)[8](https://dronelife.com/2025/06/11/european-drone-industry-readiness-2030-defence-fund-growth/).
    

## 4. Strategic Autonomy in Critical Components

**Objective**: Reduce reliance on non-EU suppliers and strengthen the European Defence Technological and Industrial Base (EDTIB).

- Commit to a 100% EU-sourced avionics and propulsion stack leveraging open-source RISC-V processors, Galileo GNSS, and domestically produced batteries—supported by Readiness 2030 grants and a dedicated “Strategic Drone Technology Roadmap” flagship under the Drone Strategy 2.0[8](https://dronelife.com/2025/06/11/european-drone-industry-readiness-2030-defence-fund-growth/)[1](https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/api/files/document/print/en/ip_22_7076/IP_22_7076_EN.pdf).
    
- Launch a cross-sectoral consortium of chip fabs, SMEs, and defence prime contractors to co-invest in pilot production lines, ensuring at least 85% BOM value of EU manufacturing by 2027.
    

## 5. Community-Centric Acceptance & Benefit-Sharing

**Objective**: Build trust, social licence, and public support for urban, semi-urban, and rural drone operations.

- Roll out “Participatory Drone Councils” in pilot regions to co-design flight corridors, privacy safeguards, and noise-monitoring dashboards that broadcast live CO₂ and decibel metrics to residents, earning ≥ 80% local approval scores[3](https://institutdelors.eu/en/publications/a-rampart-of-drones-to-defend-europe/).
    
- Introduce a “Community Benefits Fund” that allocates a share of drone-logistics service fees or defence R&D grants to local initiatives (green space, digital literacy), converting spectators into stakeholders.
    

## 6. Cross-Domain Resilience & Crisis Response Networks

**Objective**: Leverage drone fleets for both commercial logistics and emergency defence or humanitarian missions.

- Create interoperable “Droneports” with modular pads and command hubs that can be reconfigured on short notice for cargo, medical, or tactical deployments—integrating EU Satellite Centre feeds and national civil protection networks.
    
- Establish an EU Civil–Military Drone Task Force under the European Emergency Response Capacity, capable of dispatching mixed fleets within 6 hours to support disaster relief, border surveillance, or critical supply resupply in conflict-affected areas.
    

By synchronising regulatory reforms, open-architecture standards, strategic autonomy in manufacturing, AI-driven operational resilience, and deeply rooted community engagement, the EU can overcome both defense drone vulnerabilities and delivery drone limitations—positioning Europe as the global exemplar of safe, sovereign, and socially accepted unmanned aviation.

## Identified Gaps for HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11

- **Societal Readiness & Stakeholder Engagement:**  
    While AiRMOUR and SafirMed address societal readiness, many projects focus on technical demos rather than deep, ongoing community engagement and co-creation with citizens, city authorities, and logistics operators throughout the project lifecycle[3](https://www.suasnews.com/2021/02/airmour-project-opens-up-the-skies-for-medical-emergency-drones/)[8](https://www.skeyes.be/media/2106/safir-med_brochure.pdf)[4](https://www.unmannedairspace.info/latest-news-and-information/eu-funded-airmour-project-paves-way-for-flying-manned-and-unmanned-emergency-medical-vehicles/).
    
- **Scalability & Interoperability:**  
    Existing projects often target specific cities or use cases. There is a gap in demonstrating scalable, interoperable solutions that can be rapidly adapted across different urban, peri-urban, and rural environments, especially with seamless integration into existing city mobility systems[3](https://www.suasnews.com/2021/02/airmour-project-opens-up-the-skies-for-medical-emergency-drones/)[6](https://www.hamburg-logistik.net/en/our-activities/projects/medifly/)[8](https://www.skeyes.be/media/2106/safir-med_brochure.pdf).
    
- **Advanced Connectivity Solutions:**  
    Most current projects use standard communication networks (e.g., LTE/5G) for drone operations and U-space services. There is limited demonstration of next-generation, resilient, and secure communication architectures that can handle high-density, autonomous drone operations, including multi-provider interoperability and real-time data exchange[10](https://www.airbus.com/en/newsroom/stories/2020-03-connectivity-in-the-era-of-flying-taxis)[11](https://www.mdpi.com/2504-446X/8/12/702).
    
- **Autonomous Traffic Management:**  
    Traffic management in these projects often relies on predefined corridors, manual approvals, or basic deconfliction. There is a gap in deploying AI-driven, real-time, decentralized traffic management systems capable of handling dynamic, high-density, mixed-traffic environments (e.g., multi-agent reinforcement learning for airspace coordination)[12](https://www.eurekalert.org/news-releases/1049208)[13](https://www.miragenews.com/pioneering-automated-air-mobility-systems-1262025/).
    
- **Integration with Broader Urban Mobility:**  
    Many projects focus on medical or cargo use cases in isolation. There is a gap in demonstrating how air mobility services can be integrated into multimodal urban mobility platforms, including data sharing with ground transport and public transit systems[3](https://www.suasnews.com/2021/02/airmour-project-opens-up-the-skies-for-medical-emergency-drones/)[6](https://www.hamburg-logistik.net/en/our-activities/projects/medifly/)[8](https://www.skeyes.be/media/2106/safir-med_brochure.pdf).
    

## Making Your Proposal Unique

## 1. Connectivity (Communication Perspective)

- **Design a Robust, Multi-Layered Communication Architecture:**
    
    - Implement a hybrid connectivity solution combining public (5G/6G), private (dedicated mesh networks), and satellite links for redundancy and resilience.
        
    - Ensure secure, low-latency, high-availability data links for both control and payload communications, addressing cybersecurity and anti-jamming requirements[10](https://www.airbus.com/en/newsroom/stories/2020-03-connectivity-in-the-era-of-flying-taxis)[11](https://www.mdpi.com/2504-446X/8/12/702).
        
    - Enable real-time, standardized data exchange between drones, U-space/UTM, city authorities, and mobility service providers.
        
- **Interoperability & Open APIs:**
    
    - Develop open, standards-based APIs to allow seamless integration with third-party service providers, enabling a plug-and-play ecosystem for future urban air mobility expansion[10](https://www.airbus.com/en/newsroom/stories/2020-03-connectivity-in-the-era-of-flying-taxis).
        
- **Edge Computing & AI Integration:**
    
    - Use edge computing at droneports and onboard drones to process mission-critical data locally, reducing latency and supporting autonomous operations even in case of network disruptions.
        

## 2. Traffic Management Innovation

- **AI-Driven, Decentralized Traffic Management:**
    
    - Implement multi-agent reinforcement learning or similar AI systems for real-time, autonomous traffic coordination, especially at airspace intersections and merging points[12](https://www.eurekalert.org/news-releases/1049208)[13](https://www.miragenews.com/pioneering-automated-air-mobility-systems-1262025/).
        
    - Demonstrate adaptive, self-organizing airspace management that can dynamically allocate airspace and corridors based on demand, weather, and emergency priorities.
        
- **Dynamic Priority Handling:**
    
    - Integrate mechanisms for dynamic prioritization (e.g., medical emergencies, VIP transport) with automated, real-time rerouting and deconfliction.
        
- **Integration with City Mobility Platforms:**
    
    - Ensure your traffic management system exchanges data with city mobility platforms, enabling holistic, multimodal traffic optimization (air and ground).
        
- **Societal Readiness and Transparency:**
    
    - Embed citizen engagement and transparent communication into your traffic management approach, providing real-time public dashboards and feedback channels to build trust and acceptance[3](https://www.suasnews.com/2021/02/airmour-project-opens-up-the-skies-for-medical-emergency-drones/)[8](https://www.skeyes.be/media/2106/safir-med_brochure.pdf)[14](https://accelopment.com/service/proposalwriting/how-to-maximise-your-impact-on-horizon-europe-mobility-projects-tips-for-horizon-cl5-2025-01-d6-01/).

## Key Technological Gaps Between Drone Lift Air Cargo, AirMOUR, Medifly, and SafirMed Projects

Based on the available information, several significant technological gaps exist between these four drone projects, particularly in sensors, software, and onboard connectivity capabilities:

## **Platform Configuration and Payload Capabilities**

**Dronamics (Drone Lift Air Cargo)** stands out with its **Black Swan fixed-wing design** carrying **350kg payload over 2,500km range** [1](https://www.3ds.com/insights/customer-stories/dronamics-cargo-drone)[2](https://dronelife.com/2023/05/29/dronamics-cargo-drone-black-swan-can-carry-770-pounds-from-new-york-to-texas/). This represents a fundamentally different approach compared to the other projects, which focus on smaller, primarily multirotor configurations for shorter-range medical missions.

**AirMOUR** utilizes both **manned and unmanned platforms** including **Ehang 216 and Falcon L400 drones** [3](https://cordis.europa.eu/article/id/442427-validation-flights-of-life-saving-drones-launched-in-norway). The project combines passenger eVTOL, small cargo drones, and emergency medical equipment transport with payloads typically under 50kg.

**Medifly** employs a **10kg Globe UAV drone** flying at **75m altitude and 40km/h speed** [4](https://www.commercialuavnews.com/europe/german-research-project-medifly-tests-long-range-urban-medical-drone-delivery-flights) for medical sample transport over 5km distances, representing the most constrained payload and range capabilities.

**SafirMed** features **five different UAV platforms** including **passenger eVTOL, hydrogen fuel cell VTOL, battery tiltwing VTOL, AED medical drone, and X8 medical transport** [5](https://www.sesarju.eu/projects/SAFIR-Med)[6](https://cordis.europa.eu/project/id/101017701/reporting), demonstrating the most diverse technology portfolio.

## **Sensor Technology Gaps**

**LiDAR and Obstacle Detection:**

- **SafirMed** incorporates **high-precision LiDAR capturing 300,000 points per second** and **millimeter-wave radar optimized for low-visibility conditions** [7](https://dronexl.co/de/2025/06/30/dji-flycart-100-unveiled-heavy-lift-drone/), representing the most advanced sensor suite
    
- **Dronamics** relies on standard navigation sensors without advanced obstacle detection systems
    
- **AirMOUR and Medifly** use basic camera-based visual navigation systems [8](https://www.suasnews.com/2021/02/airmour-project-opens-up-the-skies-for-medical-emergency-drones/)[4](https://www.commercialuavnews.com/europe/german-research-project-medifly-tests-long-range-urban-medical-drone-delivery-flights)
    

**Navigation and Positioning:**

- **SafirMed** features **redundant GNSS (RTK ready), FLARM, and ADS-B systems** for enhanced positioning accuracy [9](https://uava.cz/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/10-agentfly-safir-med-project.pdf)
    
- **Dronamics** uses **satellite flight control system with proprietary technology** [10](https://mddb.apec.org/Documents/2023/TPTWG/AEG-TM1/23_tptwg_aeg_tm1_009.pdf)
    
- **AirMOUR** employs **standard GPS navigation** with basic telemetry [11](https://airmour.eu/project-description/)
    
- **Medifly** utilizes **conventional GPS and barometric sensors** [12](https://www.hamburg-logistik.net/en/our-activities/projects/medifly/)
    

## **Software and Autonomy Gaps**

**Artificial Intelligence Integration:**

- **SafirMed** demonstrates **advanced AI capabilities** with **multi-agent reinforcement learning** for traffic coordination and **real-time autonomous decision-making** [9](https://uava.cz/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/10-agentfly-safir-med-project.pdf)[13](https://www.skeyes.be/media/2106/safir-med_brochure.pdf)
    
- **Dronamics** implements **basic autonomous navigation** with **remote pilot oversight** [14](https://dronedj.com/2023/05/26/dronamics-test-flies-its-middle-mile-black-swan-delivery-drone/)
    
- **AirMOUR** features **limited AI functionality** focused on **mission planning and route optimization** [11](https://airmour.eu/project-description/)
    
- **Medifly** operates with **minimal autonomous features**, requiring **manual flight planning and oversight** [12](https://www.hamburg-logistik.net/en/our-activities/projects/medifly/)
    

**Flight Control Systems:**

- **SafirMed** employs **highly automated flight controllers with path control and low deviation** plus **safety functions and wind estimation** [9](https://uava.cz/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/10-agentfly-safir-med-project.pdf)
    
- **Dronamics** uses **commercial airline pilot remote control** with **basic autopilot functions** [15](https://www.prnewswire.com/in/news-releases/dronamics-cargo-drone-takes-first-flight-paving-the-way-for-the-future-of-deliveries-301834427.html)
    
- **AirMOUR** implements **standard flight control systems** with **emergency return-to-home capabilities** [8](https://www.suasnews.com/2021/02/airmour-project-opens-up-the-skies-for-medical-emergency-drones/)
    
- **Medifly** relies on **basic flight control** with **human operator supervision** [16](https://www.unmannedairspace.info/latest-news-and-information/medifly-hamburg-project-conducts-bvlos-delivers-medical-samples-between-hospitals/)
    

## **Connectivity and Communication Gaps**

**Advanced Communication Systems:**

- **SafirMed** features **redundant communication systems** including **dual mobile networks, satellite communication, and direct data links** [9](https://uava.cz/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/10-agentfly-safir-med-project.pdf)

## **Summary of Critical Technological Gaps**

The analysis reveals that **SafirMed** represents the most technologically advanced project with sophisticated sensors, AI integration, and comprehensive connectivity solutions. **Dronamics** excels in payload capacity and range but lacks advanced autonomous systems. **AirMOUR** provides balanced capabilities for emergency medical services but with limited technological sophistication. **Medifly** demonstrates the most basic technological implementation, suitable for simple medical transport tasks but lacking advanced features necessary for complex urban air mobility operations.

The most significant gaps exist in **sensor fusion capabilities**, **AI-driven autonomous decision-making**, **advanced communication redundancy**, and **comprehensive U-space integration** - areas where SafirMed leads significantly over the other projects.

## Refined Mission, Vision, and Objectives for HORIZON-CL5-2025-01-D6-11

Based on the HORIZON-CL5-2025-01-D6-11 call requirements emphasizing societal and technological readiness, here are the refined mission, vision, and objectives:

## **Mission Statement**

To develop and demonstrate a comprehensive framework for sustainable, safe, and socially accepted urban air mobility (UAM) focused on medical and logistics services through Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) operations, while establishing robust communication infrastructure and traffic management systems that integrate seamlessly with existing urban transport networks and align with EASA regulations and European values.

## **Vision Statement**

To create a resilient, smart, and citizen-centric ecosystem for innovative air mobility that bridges the gap between technological advancement and societal acceptance, enabling scalable BVLOS drone operations in European cities while fostering public trust, ensuring environmental sustainability, and supporting emergency medical services as the foundation for broader urban air mobility adoption.

## **Refined Objectives Aligned with Call Requirements**

## **1. Societal Readiness and Public Acceptance**

- **Implement comprehensive societal readiness assessment frameworks** throughout the project lifecycle, incorporating SSH (Social Sciences and Humanities) expertise to evaluate and enhance public acceptance of UAM services1[2](https://slord.sk/en/aktualita/society-driven-science-piloting-societal-readiness-in-climate-energy-mobility-projects/)
    
- **Develop evidence-based strategies for community engagement** that address citizen concerns about safety, privacy, noise, and environmental impact through co-creation processes with local authorities1[3](https://interreg-baltic.eu/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/D1.2-Social-acceptance-of-urban-air-mobility_-state-of-the-art-baseline-and-survey-development.pdf)
    
- **Create public awareness and education programs** to build trust and understanding of UAM benefits, particularly for emergency medical services and logistics applications1[4](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/cmsdata/236630/easaurbanmobilitystudy_EN.pdf)
    

## **2. Technological Infrastructure and Safety**

- **Establish next-generation communication architectures** that ensure redundant, secure, and resilient connectivity for BVLOS operations, addressing current gaps in 4G/5G coverage in peri-urban areas1[5](https://www.faa.gov/uas/advanced_operations/traffic_management)
    
- **Develop AI-driven traffic management systems** that enable real-time coordination and deconfliction in mixed airspace environments, moving beyond predefined corridors to dynamic routing1[6](https://www.autonomyglobal.co/opening-up-europes-skies-the-future-of-drone-operations-with-u-space/)
    
- **Implement advanced Detect and Avoid (DAA) capabilities** and obstacle avoidance systems through onboard sensors and edge computing integration1[7](https://publicapps.caa.co.uk/docs/33/CAP%201861%20-%20BVLOS%20Fundamentals%20v2.pdf)
    

## **3. Regulatory Compliance and Integration**

- **Ensure full compliance with EASA regulations** for certified category operations, including certification of drones, operators, and remote pilots for urban logistics and medical services1[8](https://www.uavnavigation.com/company/blog/easa-drone-regulations-comprehensive-guide-compliance)
    
- **Develop harmonized infrastructure standards** across EU member states to address current regulatory fragmentation and enable cross-border operations1[9](https://www.easa.europa.eu/en/domains/civil-drones/drones-regulatory-framework-background)
    
- **Create guidelines for U-space integration** that facilitate scalable drone operations while maintaining safety standards with existing air traffic management systems1[6](https://www.autonomyglobal.co/opening-up-europes-skies-the-future-of-drone-operations-with-u-space/)
    

## **4. Urban Integration and Multimodal Connectivity**

- **Collaborate with city authorities** to integrate UAM infrastructure (vertiports, landing areas, charging stations) into Sustainable Urban Mobility Plans (SUMPs)1[10](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11)
    
- **Assess environmental impact** including noise monitoring, microclimate effects from eVTOL operations, and energy efficiency considerations1[10](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11)
    
- **Develop multimodal transport integration** that connects UAM services with existing ground transportation networks to enhance overall urban mobility1[10](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11)
    

## **5. Business Ecosystem and Economic Viability**

- **Create sustainable business models** for UAM service providers in the logistics and medical sectors, identifying high-value use cases and market opportunities1[10](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11)
    
- **Develop roadmaps for infrastructure development** that define requirements for new or upgraded logistics and operational infrastructure1[10](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11)
    
- **Establish training and competence-building programs** for stakeholders including city authorities, operators, and regulatory bodies1[10](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11)
    

## **6. Real-world Demonstration and Validation**

- **Conduct comprehensive demonstration activities** for medical supply delivery and emergency services in urban environments with data collection and flight monitoring1[10](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11)
    
- **Validate scalability and interoperability** of the proposed solutions through pilot projects in representative urban scenarios11
    
- **Measure and quantify benefits** including impacts on traffic congestion, pollution reduction, and emergency response times1[10](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11)
    

## **Key Alignment with Call Requirements**

**Societal Readiness Pilot Focus**: The project emphasizes continuous engagement with diverse social groups and communities, integrating SSH expertise throughout all work packages to ensure responsiveness to societal needs and concerns1[2](https://slord.sk/en/aktualita/society-driven-science-piloting-societal-readiness-in-climate-energy-mobility-projects/)[11](https://prod5.assets-cdn.io/event/7855/assets/8255800755-1547560e9b.pdf).

**TRL 5 Technological Readiness**: The project targets technology validation in relevant environments while building the necessary societal acceptance framework for broader deployment1[12](https://innovationsfonden.dk/sites/default/files/2019-03/societal_readiness_levels_-_srl.pdf).

**Building on AiRMOUR**: The project leverages insights from the previous AiRMOUR project, particularly in emergency medical services applications and public engagement methodologies1[13](https://airmour.eu/project-description/)[14](https://forumvirium.fi/en/projects/the-airmour-project-increases-urban-air-mobility-competence/).

**Multi-level Governance**: The project involves city authorities, logistics operators, and regulatory bodies to ensure comprehensive stakeholder engagement and real-world applicability1[10](https://cordis.europa.eu/programme/id/HORIZON_HORIZON-CL5-2025-04-D6-11).

This refined framework positions the project to successfully address the call's emphasis on both technological innovation and societal acceptance, creating a foundation for sustainable urban air mobility deployment across European cities.

## SERA, U-space and the Port Context

## Why SERA Matters

SERA sets uniform flight rules across EU airspace. Flagship 1’s amendments will:

- Embed detect-and-avoid (DAA) alternatives to “see-and-avoid” for unmanned missions[](https://transport.ec.europa.eu/system/files/2022-11/COM_2022_652_drone_strategy_2.0.pdf).

- Define harmonised contingency procedures for drones losing C2 within a port’s CTR or maritime traffic lane, preventing “grey zones” in jurisdiction between ANSPs and VTS.

## U-Space Layers Beside VTS

Modern ports run Vessel Traffic Services. Integrating U-space service providers (USSPs) with VTS enables:

- Real-time geofencing of exclusion zones (e.g., around LNG carriers) broadcast to drones.
    
- Automated de-confliction between rotorcraft, cranes, tall-mast vessels and drone corridors.
    
- Shared spectrum (Flagship 13) for maritime AIS and U-space C-network to avoid RF clashes

What AirMOUR proposal did right 
1. **Focus on Public and Stakeholder Acceptance:** AirMour placed strong emphasis on actively measuring and improving both public and stakeholder acceptance of UAM services. Their methodology included public engagement activities, stakeholder interviews, and the analysis of perceptions and expectations regarding drone operations
2. **Addressing Societal and Functional Needs:** The proposal specifically targeted higher public acceptance for emergency medical use cases of drones and electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft.
3. **Stakeholder Engagement and Best Practices:** AirMour developed a robust stakeholder engagement program, including city authorities, aviation bodies, healthcare organizations, and the general public. The project created practical guidance and a "Guidebook for UAM Integration," emphasizing operational safety, environmental considerations, privacy, regulatory frameworks, and urban design. The guidebook outlined functional requirements for all stakeholders, such as infrastructure, vehicle capabilities, and safety protocols
4. **Demonstrations and Real-Life Testing:** A unique feature was the inclusion of real-life demonstration events in several European partner cities. These demos and simulations were shown to significantly improve public awareness and acceptance of drones and UAM solutions over time
5. **Attention to Key Success Criteria:** The project established quantifiable success criteria for each scenario tested, including safety, emissions, operational effectiveness, and integration into existing urban mobility systems.
