### IEEE Survey and Tutorials 

Ideas

To bring **novelty** to an IEEE Surveys & Tutorials journal article in the field of aerial communication and drone/UAS (including BVLOS) technologies, a submission should go beyond existing surveys by addressing _emerging disruptions, integration trends, and research gaps_ that have not yet been comprehensively reviewed. Here are concrete directions and themes that could provide significant novelty, supported by current trends and gaps in the literature:

**1. Integration of Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces (RIS) with UAV Networks**

- **Why novel:** Recent surveys highlight the transformative potential of RIS to drastically improve energy efficiency, connectivity, and coverage for UAVs, especially in urban and obstructed environments. However, a comprehensive survey that systematically covers RIS-UAV integration for BVLOS, including energy-efficient design, trajectory planning, and 6G applications, is still lacking[1](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.02162.pdf)[2](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2405959523000310).
    
- **What to cover:** Fundamentals of RIS, deployment architectures, adaptive power control, beamforming, and their implications for sustainable, scalable BVLOS operations.
    

**2. Security, Privacy, and Counter-Drone Communication in Contested Airspace**

- **Why novel:** With the rise of rogue drones, cyberattacks, and airborne espionage, airspace is becoming highly contested. There is a need for a tutorial that synthesizes the latest advances in secure, encrypted, and resilient drone communications, including anti-jamming, authentication, and counter-drone strategies for BVLOS missions[3](https://www.frost.com/growth-opportunity-news/aerospace-defense/unmanned-systems/2025s-sky-war-how-unmanned-aerial-system-uas-communication-disruptors-are-dominating-the-counter-drone-revolution-cim-ps/)[4](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/06/20/3102607/0/en/Drone-Communication-Company-Evaluation-Report-2025-DJI-RTX-and-Northrop-Grumman-Lead-the-Charge-in-Advanced-Drone-Tech.html).
    
- **What to cover:** End-to-end encryption, mesh networking for resilience, SATCOM integration, and regulatory frameworks for secure BVLOS operations.
    

**3. AI-Driven, Autonomous BVLOS Operations and Swarm Intelligence**

- **Why novel:** While AI and edge intelligence are being rapidly adopted for pathfinding, predictive maintenance, and real-time data processing, a survey that unifies these advances with communication architectures for fully autonomous, scalable BVLOS drone fleets is missing[5](https://www.dslrpros.com/blogs/rescue-drones/next-gen-uavs-cutting-edge-drone-innovations-to-watch-in-2025)[6](https://loyaltydrones.com/enterprise-drones-in-2025-innovations-trends-and-leaders/).
    
- **What to cover:** AI-powered flight control, edge computing, real-time analytics, swarm coordination, and the impact of 5G/6G for low-latency, high-reliability BVLOS missions.
    

**4. Internet of Drones (IoD) and Multi-Modal Communication Ecosystems**

- **Why novel:** The convergence of IoT, V2X, SATCOM, and terrestrial networks (5G/6G) for drones is enabling new BVLOS applications. A survey that maps out the architecture, protocols, interoperability challenges, and future research directions for this multi-modal ecosystem would fill a critical gap[7](https://startup.info/7-technology-innovations-that-revolutionize-drone-survey-services/)[8](https://www.technology-innovators.com/drone-integration-with-internet-of-things-iot-enabling-smart-and-connected-environments/)[4](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/06/20/3102607/0/en/Drone-Communication-Company-Evaluation-Report-2025-DJI-RTX-and-Northrop-Grumman-Lead-the-Charge-in-Advanced-Drone-Tech.html).
    
- **What to cover:** Interoperability, real-time data fusion, IoT-enabled drones, hybrid communication systems, and their role in smart cities, logistics, and emergency response.
    

**5. Energy-Efficient, Sustainable Operations and Low-Altitude Economy (LAE)**

- **Why novel:** As the drone economy expands, sustainability is a major concern. A survey focusing on energy harvesting, battery management, and green communication protocols for persistent BVLOS operations—especially with RIS and AI optimization—would be timely and impactful[1](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.02162.pdf).
    
- **What to cover:** Energy-efficient routing, resource allocation, environmental impact, and regulatory considerations for sustainable drone networks.
    

**6. Multi-UAV Collaborative Sensing and Cooperative Communication**

- **Why novel:** Most current works focus on single UAVs or basic multi-UAV coordination. A comprehensive review of collaborative sensing, joint task allocation, and cooperative communication (such as virtual MIMO and CoMP in the sky) for BVLOS and large-scale missions would provide new insights[9](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2207.04498.pdf)[10](https://cdnsciencepub.com/doi/10.1139/dsa-2023-0079).
    
- **What to cover:** Multi-UAV task allocation, distributed MIMO, mesh and hierarchical mesh networks, and their applications in disaster response and industrial monitoring.
    

**7. Regulatory, Standardization, and Airspace Integration for BVLOS**

- **Why novel:** The regulatory landscape is evolving rapidly, but few surveys provide a global, up-to-date synthesis of standards, airspace integration (U-space, UTM), and certification processes for BVLOS drone communications[4](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/06/20/3102607/0/en/Drone-Communication-Company-Evaluation-Report-2025-DJI-RTX-and-Northrop-Grumman-Lead-the-Charge-in-Advanced-Drone-Tech.html).
    
- **What to cover:** Comparative analysis of regulatory frameworks, challenges in harmonizing standards, and the impact on communication technology adoption.
    

**Summary Table: Novelty Directions for IEEE Surveys & Tutorials**

|Theme|Novelty Rationale|Key Aspects to Cover|
|---|---|---|
|RIS-UAV Integration|Emerging, under-surveyed|Energy efficiency, 6G, trajectory planning|
|Security & Counter-Drone|Rising threats, new tech|Encryption, mesh, SATCOM, regulation|
|AI/Autonomous BVLOS|Rapid AI adoption|Edge intelligence, swarm, 5G/6G|
|IoD & Multi-Modal Comms|Ecosystem convergence|IoT, V2X, SATCOM, interoperability|
|Sustainability/LAE|Green operations|Energy harvesting, battery, policy|
|Multi-UAV Collaboration|Advanced coordination|Virtual MIMO, mesh, disaster response|
|Regulation & Standards|Fast-changing, fragmented|Global frameworks, UTM, certification|

A submission addressing one or more of these themes with a **comprehensive, up-to-date, and critical synthesis**—especially with a focus on integration, cross-disciplinary trends, and open research challenges—would stand out as novel and highly relevant for IEEE Surveys & Tutorials[11](https://pure.qub.ac.uk/en/publications/aeronautical-networks-for-in-flight-connectivity-a-tutorial-of-th)[1](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.02162.pdf)[4](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/06/20/3102607/0/en/Drone-Communication-Company-Evaluation-Report-2025-DJI-RTX-and-Northrop-Grumman-Lead-the-Charge-in-Advanced-Drone-Tech.html).

**Key Themes Across Surveys**

1. **Spectrum & Channel Modeling**  
    – Aerial networks demand dedicated or dynamically allocated spectrum to counter interference and scarcity.  
    – Existing 2-D channel models and propagation assumptions are inadequate; 3-D, altitude-dependent models are needed, especially in urban, suburban, and contested environments.
    
2. **Mobility & Topology Management**  
    – Rapid 3-D mobility and frequent topology changes require new mobility models, routing protocols, and DTN frameworks tailored to UAV‐specific dynamics and safety constraints.
    
3. **Protocol Stack Adaptations**  
    – PHY layer: mobility-aware rate adaptation; error‐resilient LoS/non-LoS designs.  
    – MAC layer: latency-sensitive access for URLLC; link-failure detection and recovery for DTN.  
    – Network layer: geo-routing and hybrid ferry/relay strategies; scalable mesh and cellular integration.  
    – Transport layer: flow control under intermittent connectivity; application-aware multi-QoS scheduling.
    
4. **Energy, SWAP & Autonomy**  
    – UAV flight time and payload constraints call for joint energy-aware communication and trajectory optimization solutions, including AI-driven real-time control.
    
5. **Security & Safety**  
    – Lightweight authentication, intrusion detection, and anti-jamming measures are critical, especially with increasing UAV densities and autonomy.  
    – Coordinated counter-UAV defenses and secure swarm communication remain under-explored.
    
6. **Future Paradigms (5G→6G)**  
    – Integration of AI, RIS, cell-free massive MIMO, and THz communications for enhanced aerial link reliability and capacity.  
    – Convergence of radio sensing and localization with communications for robust UAV control and situational awareness.
    

By addressing these gaps—ranging from fundamental channel modeling to AI-driven network orchestration and security—future research can enable robust, scalable, and secure UAV-based communication systems that meet the stringent requirements of civil, commercial, and defense applications through 2025 and beyond.

# Suitability of U-Space and UTM Communication Research for IEEE Communications Surveys & Tutorials

The **IEEE Communications Surveys & Tutorials** journal aims to publish comprehensive, in-depth overviews of communications topics, emphasizing theoretical foundations, protocols, architectures, and emerging challenges. Below is an assessment of the current state of research on U-Space and UTM (Unmanned Aircraft System Traffic Management) communications and its fit with this journal’s scope.

## 1. Landscape of U-Space and UTM Communication Research

1.1 U-Space Services Implementation Monitoring

- Eurocontrol’s U-Space implementation reports detail the maturity of digitalized U-Space services—registration, identification, geo-awareness, and tactical conflict resolution—across EU Member States [1](https://www.eurocontrol.int/sites/default/files/2022-11/eurocontrol-u-space-services-implementation-monitoring-report-2022.pdf).
    
- These reports quantify deployment status (U1–U3 phases) but offer limited technical depth on underlying communication protocols, channel models, or security mechanisms.
    

1.2 Concept of Operations and Architectures

- The SESAR CORUS ConOps (4th ed.) and NASA’s UTM fact sheets outline high-level architectures, service layers (flight planning, conformance monitoring, separation assurance), and prototype systems [2](https://www.nasa.gov/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/utm-factsheet-11-05-15.pdf?emrc=a0f68a)[3](https://www.sesarju.eu/sites/default/files/documents/reports/U-space%20CONOPS%204th%20edition.pdf).
    
- Academic surveys (e.g., McCarthy et al.) explore urban UTM fundamentals—airspace modeling, dynamic geo-fencing, scalable traffic management—and preliminary communication designs for low-altitude operations [4](https://mural.maynoothuniversity.ie/id/eprint/13145/1/TM-Fundamental-2020.pdf).
    

1.3 Security and Networking Surveys

- Recent surveys cover security in broader UTM contexts, including cryptographic frameworks for command-and-control links, intrusion detection, and secure spectrum sharing [5](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2211.05640.pdf).
    
- Other works focus on integrating 5G cellular networks into UTM/U-Space, addressing QoS, latency, coverage, and interference in urban scenarios [6](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2411.09666).
    
- However, there is no single survey consolidating communication channel modeling, protocol stack adaptations, performance analysis, and security for U-Space/UTM.
    

## 2. Gaps and Need for a Dedicated COMST Survey

- **Lack of Unified Communication-Focused Overview**: Existing reports and surveys address individual aspects (implementation status, high-level architectures, security, cellular integration) but do not synthesize:  
    – End-to-end communication protocols (C2, telemetry, data links)  
    – Channel models for low-altitude, urban, and rural UTM environments  
    – Cross-layer design (MAC, routing, transport) under UAV mobility and SWAP constraints  
    – Security/privacy mechanisms tailored to U-Space service interfaces
    
- **Emerging Technologies Under-Explored**: While cell-free massive MIMO, RIS, THz links, and AI-driven orchestration appear in 6G UAV surveys, their relevance to U-Space/UTM (e.g., for dynamic conflict resolution or separation assurance) lacks dedicated treatment.
    
- **Standardization and Interoperability**: ConOps documents describe service definitions, but communication standards (3GPP UAS integration, IEEE U-Space transceiver interfaces) remain scattered across multiple venues.
    

## 3. Alignment with IEEE Communications Surveys & Tutorials

A **COMST** article on U-Space/UTM communications would ideally:

– Survey and compare channel modeling efforts for UAV-ground, UAV-UTM server, and inter-UAV links, including urban microcell, suburban, and open-field scenarios.  
– Review protocol adaptations across PHY, MAC, network, and transport layers to accommodate high mobility, 3-D topology changes, and heterogeneous traffic (C2, payload data, surveillance feeds).  
– Analyze performance trade-offs in SWAP-constrained UAV networks, including energy-aware scheduling and trajectory-coupled resource allocation.  
– Catalogue security frameworks—lightweight authentication, intrusion detection for U-Space services, anti-jamming, secure swarm coordination.  
– Detail integration with existing cellular/NTN standards (3GPP Rel-17/Rel-18 UAS support, IEEE U-Space interfaces), highlighting gaps and research directions.

Such a tutorial-style survey, blending theory (channel and protocol models), system design (architecture and standards), and forward-looking challenges (6G enablers), matches the **IEEE COMST** mandate of authoritative, tutorial-level coverage of communications research.

# Key Communication Challenges Revealed by Recent U-Space Implementation Reports

The latest U-Space implementation reports—from EUROCONTROL’s 2022 Services Implementation Monitoring, EASA’s U-WELCOME findings, and EASA’s inspections roadmap—highlight several **recurring communication challenges** that must be addressed to achieve reliable, scalable U-Space services.

## 1. Interoperability of Data Exchanges

Despite a well-defined service framework, **inconsistent data models and protocols** persist between U-Space Service Providers (USSPs) and Common Information Service Providers (CISPs).

- EUROCONTROL notes that consolidation around a single digital format (e.g., ED-269) has advanced, yet many States still report “planned” rather than deployed geo-awareness and surveillance data exchange services[1](https://www.eurocontrol.int/sites/default/files/2022-11/eurocontrol-u-space-services-implementation-monitoring-report-2022.pdf).
    
- EASA’s U-WELCOME project found that USSPs in shared airspace struggle to exchange telemetry seamlessly, due to divergent RESTful API implementations and varying update cycles—jeopardizing coordinated conflict detection across providers[2](https://www.sesarju.eu/news/solving-big-challenges-small-packets-sesar-project-makes-progress-u-space-information).
    

## 2. Network Capacity and Latency

U-Space services require **real-time, high-fidelity communication** for flight authorizations, geo-fencing updates, and tactical conflict alerts.

- The SESAR IMPETUS trial emphasized that legacy “monolithic” architectures cannot handle the data volume; only microservices platforms demonstrated the capacity to deliver sub-second updates across wide areas[3](https://www.easa.europa.eu/en/newsroom-and-events/press-releases/easa-certifies-anra-technologies-first-u-space-service-provider).
    
- EUROCONTROL’s monitoring report flags **communication infrastructure monitoring** as one of the lowest-maturity U2 services (only 4% completion)[1](https://www.eurocontrol.int/sites/default/files/2022-11/eurocontrol-u-space-services-implementation-monitoring-report-2022.pdf), underscoring underinvestment in network resilience and low-latency links.
    

## 3. Standardization of Interfaces

Diverse national approaches to **remote identification (Remote ID), flight plan processing**, and command-and-control (C2) links hamper harmonization.

- EASA’s planned inspections in 2025 identified major divergences in Remote ID standards, onboarding processes for USSP certification, and C2 cybersecurity requirements—leading to potential gaps in cross-border drone operations[4](https://www.eurocontrol.int/tool/u-space-services-implementation-monitoring-tool).
- The U-Space Concept of Operations (CONOPS) 4th edition calls for common negotiation processes for strategic conflict resolution, but no standardized protocol exists, leaving U2 tactical geo-fencing vulnerable to inconsistent implementations[5](https://www.sesarju.eu/sites/default/files/documents/reports/U-space%20CONOPS%204th%20edition.pdf).

## 4. Scalability and Security

Supporting **dense, multi-provider** U-Space corridors demands robust, scalable communication architectures that preserve confidentiality and integrity.

- EUROCONTROL’s U-WELCOME workshops reported challenges in scaling U1/U2 services: ensuring **API rate-limits**, message authentication, and secure token management for hundreds of simultaneous drone connections[6](https://www.unmannedairspace.info/latest-news-and-information/implementing-u-space-in-europe-easa-outlines-new-measures-to-speed-adoption/).
    
- EASA highlights the need for **business continuity** and cybersecurity frameworks integrated into USSP certification, but many Members lack mature intrusion-detection or anti-jamming solutions for low-altitude data links[4](https://www.eurocontrol.int/tool/u-space-services-implementation-monitoring-tool).
    

**Conclusion**  
To overcome these communication challenges, stakeholders must converge on unified data models, adopt microservices-based architectures, accelerate deployment of resilient networks (including 5G/NTN), and harmonize interface standards—ensuring U-Space can safely support high-density drone operations across Europe.
